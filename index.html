Introduction¶

Terrorism is an issue that many countries and governments face when trying to maintain peace within their country. Terrorist groups can form for many reasons, traditionally stemming from the extreme radicalization of certain beliefs. This process of radicalization eventually leads to a plethora of terrorist attacks. While the general goal of an attack is to gain attention for the group and to inflict harm on a population, these attacks can be of various magnitudes, can be of different types, and can have various other factors that set them apart from each other. The Global Terrorism Database helps to classify over 180,000 attacks that occurred from 1970 to 2017.

For our final project, we decided to use the Global Terrorism Database in order to figure out whether or not we could predict whether or not an attack was classified as a success. While there are many potential metrics that can determine whether or not it was successful, the database comes with a column that states whether or not the success was successful. This database kept track of and itemized every terrorist attack from the years 1970 - 2017. We were interested in whether or not we could predict whether or not an attack would be successful, and that is what we decided to base our project on.
